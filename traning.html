PART 1 Required

🏆 The Golden Trio

Three pages. Every client. No exceptions. These three pages work together as a system to transform a complete stranger into a hot, qualified lead. Miss one and the machine breaks.

🎬 The Analogy The Golden Trio is like a 3-act movie. Act 1 (Index) makes them feel seen. Act 2 (Services) makes them believe. Act 3 (About Us) makes them trust. You skip an act? The audience walks out confused.

  • ✓
    I know the goal of these pages isn't aesthetics — it's qualified leads Beauty is a side-effect. Conversion is the mission.
  • ✓
    I understand the 3-act psychological sequence for visitors Feel seen (Index) → Believe in the solution (Services) → Trust the provider (About Us)
PAGE 1 Required

🪝 The Index Page

The homepage is a precision psychological funnel. Every element appears in a specific, non-negotiable order. This is not creative freedom territory — this is architecture.

🎯
Core Mission Turn a completely unknown visitor into someone who says "This is exactly what I need — where do I sign?" before they scroll to the bottom of the page.
🎣

Step 1 — The Hook

The very first sentence on screen. Your make-or-break moment.

▶
🎬 The Analogy The Hook is the movie trailer. If the trailer is boring, nobody buys a ticket. If the hook doesn't grab in 3 seconds, they're gone forever. You will never get that attention back.
  • 1

    Call out the exact avatar by name

    Name WHO this page is for. Be specific — vague hooks convert nobody.

    ✦ "Строители в Пловдив" / "Зъболекари в София"
  • 2

    OR show an absurd, irresistible result

    Lead with a shocking outcome that makes the right person stop and go "Wait, what?"

    ✦ "100% завършен дом за 30 дни — гарантирано"
  • 3

    Run the 1-second clarity test

    Show the hook to someone unfamiliar. They should know exactly who it's for within 1 second. Confused? Rewrite.

  • ✓
    Hook names the specific avatar OR shows a dramatic result
  • ✓
    Hook passes the 1-second clarity test with a real person
🔥

Step 2 — Agitate the Problem

Make them feel deeply understood. Use their words, not yours.

▶
💬 The Analogy Think of this like a doctor who describes your exact symptoms before you even open your mouth. You immediately think: "This person gets it. They know my problem." That instant trust is what the Agitate section creates.
  • 1

    Research how THEY describe their own problem

    Read their reviews, Facebook groups, forum posts. Steal their exact words and phrases. Verbatim.

  • 2

    Write it so they say "That's EXACTLY me!"

    Not "yeah, I guess that's sort of relatable." Full recognition. They should feel read.

  • 3

    Do NOT offer the solution yet

    Let the pain breathe. Build the emotional pressure first. Solution comes in the next step.

  • ✓
    Copy uses the client's own vocabulary, not marketing speak
  • ✓
    Pain is fully described before any solution is offered
💎

Step 3 — The Grand Slam Offer

Sell the vacation, not the plane. Hormozi's core framework.

▶
✈️ The Analogy Nobody buys a flight because they love sitting in a metal tube for 8 hours. They buy the beach. The family reunion. The freedom. Your offer must sell that dream outcome — not the technical steps to get there.
  • 1

    Lead with the Dream Outcome

    What is the ultimate result the client's customer actually wants? Lead with that — not the service.

    ✦ "Пълен дом на ключ — без стрес, без изненади"
  • 2

    Remove the effort and sacrifice

    Show them they won't have to do the hard stuff. You handle it. They just get the result.

  • 3

    Compress the time delay — make it feel fast

    "Results in 30 days" beats "as quickly as possible" every single time. Be specific.

  • 4

    Raise the Perceived Likelihood of Achievement

    Show proof the result is actually achievable — photos, numbers, testimonials. Make belief easy.

🧮
The Hormozi Value Equation — Memorize This Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Effort + Time Delay). Maximize the top. Minimize the bottom. That's the entire formula for an irresistible offer.
  • ✓
    Offer leads with Dream Outcome, not features or process steps
  • ✓
    Offer removes the effort and sacrifice from the client
  • ✓
    Timeline stated is specific and feels fast
⭐

Step 4 — Social Proof

People follow people. 3–5 real client screenshots. No stock photos. Ever.

▶
🍕 The Analogy You walk past two pizza joints. One is empty. One has a line out the door. Which one do you choose? The line. Every time. Social proof is that line — without the wait.
  • 1

    Use 3–5 real client testimonials

    WhatsApp/Messenger/Google screenshots are more believable than polished design cards. Keep them raw.

  • 2

    Pick testimonials that address top objections

    If clients worry about price, find a testimonial that says "worth every lev." Make proof do double duty.

  • 3

    Show full names and faces when possible

    Real people with real names and real photos = maximum trust. Anonymous = suspicious.

  • ✓
    At least 3 real testimonials with identifiable names are present
  • ✓
    At least 1 testimonial addresses a common objection
🚀

Step 5 — The Call to Action

One action. One exit. Ethical urgency. Absolute clarity.

▶
🚦 The Analogy If a traffic light showed red, yellow, and green all at once — nobody would move. One signal. One action. A confused visitor does nothing. Give them one door, make it irresistible.
  • 1

    ONE button. ONE action. Remove everything else.

    No "follow us / subscribe / book / call / email" — pick one, delete all others. Be ruthless.

  • 2

    Use Ethical Urgency and Scarcity

    Must be 100% true. Fake scarcity destroys trust permanently when discovered. Never fake it.

    ✦ "Приемаме само 5 нови партньора този месец. Запази час!"
  • 3

    Button copy must be action-oriented

    "Запази час" beats "Submit" every time. The button should feel like the start of a journey.

  • ✓
    Exactly ONE CTA exists on the entire page
  • ✓
    Urgency and scarcity used is 100% truthful and ethical
  • ✓
    Button copy is action-oriented, not generic ("Submit", "Click Here")
PAGE 2 Required

📦 The Services / Products Page

This page reframes the purchase from a "cost" into an "investment" and makes the decision feel obvious, not risky.

📊

The 3-Tier Package Structure

Always three levels. Always. Non-negotiable architecture.

▶
☕ The Analogy Starbucks doesn't offer one size. Small, Medium, Large. Most people pick Medium — because compared to Small it feels reasonable, and compared to Large it feels smart. You're engineering the choice, not just listing options.
Tier Name Example Strategic Purpose
Tier 1 МинимумEntry-level access Anchors the price. Makes Tier 2 feel cheap by comparison.
Tier 2 GrowthMost popular ★ The sweet spot. Most clients will choose this. Design it as the obvious "smart" choice.
Tier 3 DominationPremium all-in Makes Tier 2 feel reasonable. Some clients will pick this. Pure margin.
  • ✓
    Services page has exactly 3 clearly distinct tiers
  • ✓
    Middle tier is visually highlighted as "most popular"
🧮

ROI Math — Justify the Price

Hormozi's method: show the math so the price feels like a bargain.

▶
🎣 The Analogy Nobody thinks a fishing rod is expensive once they can see the fish it will catch. Show the fish first. Then the price of the rod feels small.
  • 1

    Find out what one client is worth to them

    Ask: "How much does one new customer bring you on average?" Get the number. It's your weapon.

  • 2

    Show the math publicly on the page

    Exactly how many new clients does it take to pay back the investment? Make it feel tiny.

    ✦ 3,850 лв. ÷ 1,000 лв./клиент = само 4 клиента за цяла година
  • 3

    Frame everything after that as pure profit

    "Client #5 and beyond? That's pure profit for you." Reframes cost as leverage, permanently.

  • ✓
    ROI calculation is visibly shown on the Services page
  • ✓
    Price is framed as investment, not expense
😱

Cost of Inaction — The Nightmare Scenario

What happens if they don't buy? Paint it clearly and honestly.

▶
🏥 The Analogy A doctor doesn't just say "this pill will help you." They say: "Without it, here's what happens to your body over the next 6 months." Both are honest. One is just much more motivating.
  • 1

    Describe the nightmare 6 months from now

    No website? Competitors eat their lunch. Still on referrals? Revenue stays unpredictable. Be specific.

  • 2

    Be honest — never manipulative

    Only describe real, genuine consequences of inaction. We are ethical. We are just direct. There is a difference.

  • ✓
    Cost of inaction is clearly described on the page
  • ✓
    Consequences described are 100% honest and accurate

🧠 The About Us Page

This page builds iron authority. No clichés. No "young and dynamic team." Just credibility, story, and trust — in that order.

The Origin Story (Far Past → Present)

Character through struggle. The 3-act origin arc.

▶
🎬 The Analogy Every superhero movie starts with the origin story. Batman didn't start as Batman. The struggle makes the hero believable and trustworthy. Your client's story of hardship is their greatest credibility asset.
  • 1

    Far Past — Where did they come from?

    The humble beginning. The harder times. The context that makes their expertise feel earned, not inherited.

  • 2

    Recent Past — What changed?

    The turning point. The breakthrough moment. What made them commit to this work fully.

  • 3

    Present — Why does this matter for the visitor?

    Connect their journey to the visitor's problem. "This is why we're uniquely positioned to help you."

  • ✓
    About page tells a real, specific origin story that includes struggle
  • ✓
    Story connects back to the visitor's specific problem at the end
  • ✓
    "Млад и динамичен екип" or any equivalent cliché is completely absent
🏅

Authority Signals — Stack the Credibility

Certifications, client counts, years of experience, measurable results.

▶
  • 1

    Quantify everything possible

    "5+ years experience" is okay. "83 clients served, 0 refunds" is irresistible. Numbers beat adjectives.

  • 2

    Show certifications and partnerships

    Logos of known organizations, official certifications, industry memberships — all build trust instantly and visually.

  • 3

    Put real faces and names front and center

    An "anonymous company" is scary. A real human with a name and a face is trustworthy. Always humanize.

  • ✓
    At least 3 quantified authority signals (numbers, stats, client count)
  • ✓
    Real photos and names of team / owner are present on the page
PART 2 Optional / Niche-Based

🧩 Dynamic Modules

Add these modules based on the client's specific business model. They remove the remaining friction — hesitation, confusion, fear — that stops a warm visitor from converting.

🚗 The Analogy The Golden Trio is the car. These modules are optional features — sunroof, GPS, heated seats. Not every car needs all of them. But when a client needs one, not including it is leaving money on the table.
📅 When to add

Booking / Calendar System

For time-based businesses: dentists, hotels, academies, consultants. Must allow booking in ≤3 clicks without leaving the page.

📸 When to add

Before / After Gallery

For visual results: interior design, construction, dental aesthetics. Each photo must tell a mini success story. Massively raises Perceived Likelihood.

🛡️ Always — near final CTA

FAQ + Risk Reversal

Kill every objection before they voice it. Include a bold, specific guarantee. "We take the risk — not you." e.g. 30-day 100% money-back.

⚠️
FAQ + Risk Reversal is Practically Mandatory Even if listed as "optional" — almost every project benefits from this module placed just above the final CTA. It is the last objection-killer before someone either clicks or leaves forever. Treat it as default.
  • ✓
    I've identified which modules apply to my current client's niche Time-based? → Calendar. Visual results? → Before/After. Everyone? → FAQ + Risk Reversal.
  • ✓
    Booking system (if used) allows reservation in ≤3 clicks, no page change
  • ✓
    Before/After photos (if used) each tell a short transformation story
  • ✓
    FAQ answers the top 5 objections the client's customers actually have Ask the client: "What questions do people ask before they decide?" Use those exact questions.
  • ✓
    Guarantee is bold, specific, and places all the risk on us — not the prospect
PART 3 Run Before Every Launch

✅ The 5 Merciless QA Questions

No website goes live until it answers YES to all 5. This is your pre-flight checklist. Run it on every single project, every single time. Click each one as you verify it.

🛑
Absolute Rule A single "No" means the site does NOT launch. Fix it first. No exceptions. No "we'll fix it after launch." No client pressure overrides this checklist.
01

Is there ONLY ONE clear Call to Action?

A confused mind doesn't buy. Exactly one desired action must exist. Remove every other button, link, or distraction from the page.

✅
02

Does the machine load lightning fast? (95+ PageSpeed)

Every 1 second of delay loses 20% of visitors. Run PageSpeed Insights. If below 95 — optimize before launch. No exceptions.

✅
03

Are we selling the vacation, not the airplane?

Is the entire site focused on the Dream Outcome — or are we listing boring technical specs and features? If it reads like a brochure, rewrite it entirely.

✅
04

Is the risk fully reversed?

Is there a clear, strong, visible guarantee that makes a prospect feel foolish for refusing? If they still carry the risk — the guarantee is not strong enough.

✅
05

Does it pass the 3rd-Grade Test?

Can a random person understand what we sell and what to do next within 5 seconds? If it's complicated — simplify the copy. No jargon. No walls of text.

✅

🎉 Machine Cleared for Launch

All 5 QA gates passed. This digital machine is ready to generate revenue. Ship it.

REFERENCE

📖 Glossary — Hormozi Terms You Must Know Cold

These terms appear in every project brief, client call, and internal review. Know them without thinking.

Dream Outcome
The ultimate result the client's customer actually wants in their life. Not the service — the transformation. Always lead with this.
Grand Slam Offer
An offer so good and so de-risked that the prospect feels foolish saying no. High value, low effort, fast result, zero risk for the buyer.
Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
How confident the visitor feels that THEY will get the promised result. Testimonials, before/after, case studies — all raise this number.
Time Delay
How long until the customer gets the result? Shorter = more valuable. "Results in 30 days" always beats "as soon as possible."
Risk Reversal
A guarantee that transfers all risk from the buyer to the seller. Makes "no" feel irrational. Must be specific and bold, not vague and forgettable.
Ethical Urgency / Scarcity
A real, truthful reason to act now. Limited spots, genuine deadline. NEVER fake this — it destroys trust permanently if the prospect discovers the lie.
Friction
Anything that makes it harder for a visitor to convert: extra clicks, confusing copy, slow loading, multiple CTAs, missing trust signals. Remove all of it.
Avatar
The specific, exact type of customer the client wants to attract. The hook must call out this avatar by name or clear implication.
Cost of Inaction
What happens to the prospect if they do nothing. The nightmare scenario. Describing it honestly is ethical; ignoring it is a wasted persuasion opportunity.
The 3rd-Grade Test
Could a child understand what this page sells and what to do in 5 seconds? If not — the copy is too complex. Simplify ruthlessly.
